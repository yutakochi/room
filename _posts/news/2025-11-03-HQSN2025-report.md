It was my first time visiting Shimane Prefecture, so I also visited Izumo Taisha (出雲大社), one of Japan's most historic and significant shrines. According to tradition, all the gods in Japan gather at this shrine in October. This is why this month is called *Kamiari-zuki* (神在月, literally “month with gods”), while in other regions it is called *Kannazuki* (神無月, “month without gods”).

The nearest station to Izumo Taisha is currently Izumo Taisha-mae Station (出雲大社前駅). In the past, there was the JR Taisha Station (JR大社前駅), which was designed in a traditional Japanese style to match the architecture of Izumo Taisha. It is the only station building of its kind in Japan designated as a cultural property.
